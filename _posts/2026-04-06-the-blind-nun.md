# The Blind Nun

When the Buddha was in this world, there was a blind nun.

Though she could not see, she used her hands to sew her robe. One day, the thread slipped from the needle, and she said, "Who will benefit me by threading this needle?"

The Buddha came to her and said, "I love beneficial merit. Let me thread the needle for you."

Recognizing his voice, she bowed and said, "The Buddha is complete with merit. Why do you say you love merit?"

The Buddha replied, "Though I am complete with merit, I deeply understand its cause, effect, and power. It is because of merit that I excel among all beings. Therefore, I cherish it."

Then he said, "I always make an effort. That is why I attained complete enlightenment."

At that moment, the nun attained the dharma eye—and could see.
